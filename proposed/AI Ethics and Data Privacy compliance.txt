**Executive Summary**

The paper addresses the ethical and privacy concerns associated with the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in digital media systems, particularly the spread of misinformation and fake news. It highlights the need for robust privacy protection measures to safeguard citizens' rights and freedoms, as emphasized by EU legislative documents. The focus is on the challenges posed by the rapid evolution of technology and the potential risks to democratic processes.

The paper identifies several key issues, including the difficulty in demonstrating respect for ethical principles and the limitations of data storage. These challenges hinder the development of AI systems that can be effectively managed and controlled. The GDPR, implemented in Romania starting May 25, 2018, is a critical framework for ensuring compliance with data protection regulations, emphasizing the importance of transparency and user consent.

The paper also discusses the impact of AI on societal behavior and attitudes, suggesting that AI systems can either positively or negatively influence user behavior. The ethical implications of AI in various sectors, such as health, education, and defense, are explored, underscoring the need for comprehensive ethical guidelines and regulatory compliance.

The paper concludes by emphasizing the necessity of addressing new ethical issues and safety concerns related to AI, as well as the importance of human oversight and control in these systems. It advocates for the development of AI systems that are both beneficial and ethically sound, with a focus on regulatory compliance and the protection of individual rights. Future research directions include the integration of AI into existing systems and the validation of new ethical frameworks against real-world scenarios.
