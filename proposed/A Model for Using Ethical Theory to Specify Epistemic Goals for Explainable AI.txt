**Executive Summary**

This paper explores the ethical considerations of AI (Artificial Intelligence) explainability, particularly focusing on the moral accountability of AI systems. The authors argue that AI, especially those capable of complex decision-making, may eventually possess moral status, akin to humans, which would necessitate their accountability for their actions. However, the increasing complexity of AI systems makes it challenging to assess their moral status, leading to a "responsibility gap."

The paper introduces the concept of XAI (Explainable AI) as a means to address this gap. XAI aims to provide insights into how AI systems arrive at their decisions, thereby enabling humans to understand and evaluate their actions. The authors propose that XAI should meet specific epistemic goals, including providing sufficient information about the AI's decision-making processes, enabling users to assess the AI's motivations, and ensuring that the AI's actions align with moral principles.

The paper draws on Nomy Arpaly's account of moral-worthiness, which distinguishes between praise-worthy and blameworthy entities. Applying Arpaly's framework to AI, the authors identify three key requirements for XAI: (1) providing relevant moral reasons for the AI's actions, (2) enabling users to assess the AI's motivations, and (3) ensuring that the AI's actions are morally justifiable. These requirements are derived from the need to establish whether AI can be held accountable for their actions, a critical issue as AI systems become more sophisticated and autonomous.

The authors conclude that AI explainability is crucial for addressing the moral accountability of AI systems and closing the responsibility gap. By making AI decision-making processes transparent and accessible, XAI tools can facilitate the assessment of AI moral status and ensure that AI systems act in accordance with moral principles. The paper emphasizes the importance of these epistemic goals in the development of AI systems that can be held accountable for their actions, thereby contributing to a more ethical and responsible use of AI technology.
