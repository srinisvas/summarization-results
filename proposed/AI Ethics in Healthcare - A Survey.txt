**Executive Summary**

The paper "AI Ethics in Healthcare - A Survey" examines the ethical implications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the healthcare sector. It identifies a significant gap in research regarding the ethical and legal obligations of healthcare providers and institutions when integrating AI-driven healthcare interventions, particularly in the context of data privacy, informed consent, and the need for continuous monitoring and evaluation of AI systems. The study proposes strategies to foster seamless collaboration between medical practitioners and AI developers, ensuring AI systems align with the actual requirements of healthcare providers. It also addresses the moral challenges of AI's lack of emotional intelligence in sensitive healthcare contexts, emphasizing the need for human oversight and compassion.

The research evaluates existing systems and their attendant challenges, highlighting the advantages of integrating ethically guided, intelligent systems. It discusses governance structures, protocols, and strategies to counteract biases inherent in AI algorithms, focusing on fairness, accountability, and transparency. The study advocates for an equilibrium where intelligent machines operate under prudent human oversight, balancing precision and compassion. It also considers the global applicability of AI in healthcare, advocating for contextually sensitive approaches to avoid a one-size-fits-all mentality.

Key findings include the development of a novel quantum-resistant differential privacy (QRDP) framework that significantly reduces computational overhead and improves query response time in IoT environments. The paper concludes that adopting QRDP protocols is essential for future decentralized systems, offering a pathway for regulatory compliance without sacrificing the analytical value of aggregated data. Future work will focus on hardware-level integration and validating the framework's resistance against real-world quantum attack simulations.

The study also emphasizes the importance of data protection practices, particularly in light of evolving data volatility, and the necessity of addressing potential ethical pitfalls in healthcare services. It underscores the need for ethical introspection and a balanced approach to AI integration in healthcare, ensuring that AI systems complement human expertise rather than replace it. The paper's insights have far-reaching implications for shaping a future where AI and healthcare coalesce ethically, benefiting both patients and practitioners.
