The paper introduces a novel privacy leakage-tolerance based noise enhancing strategy (PTNES) for improving the efficiency of noise obfuscation in cloud computing. The strategy builds upon existing noise generation strategies by creating a noise generation set based on the customer-defined privacy leakage-tolerance. This set helps in reducing the cost of noise obfuscation while maintaining a reasonable effectiveness of privacy protection. The simulation results show that PTERNES can significantly improve the efficiency of noise obfuscation by decreasing the noise cost. The authors propose a privacy leakage-tolerance based noise generation set creation model (PTNCM) to guide and manage noise generation processes, enhancing the existing noise obfuscation strategies. They also discuss the privacy leakage risk evaluation under noise obfuscation and present a privacy leakage-tolerance based noise generation set creation algorithm (PTNCA) to create the noise generation set. Overall, PTERNES addresses the privacy leakage concerns in cloud computing by considering the customer-defined boundary and improving the efficiency of noise obfuscation. Future work can include further evaluation of PTERNES in different scenarios and exploring its integration with other privacy protection techniques.}
