The paper discusses the ethical implications and regulatory challenges of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the context of data privacy and human rights. It highlights the EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) as a key framework for protecting personal data, emphasizing the need for a unified legislative approach to AI. The paper presents a comparative analysis of AI ethics in the EU and the US, along with the OECD's recommendations, and examines the tension between ethical and unethical aspects of AI, particularly in terms of human rights and data privacy. It concludes by advocating for a collaborative effort to establish a universally accepted regulatory framework for AI to ensure respect for human rights and privacy.}
