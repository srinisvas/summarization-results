The paper provides a detailed analysis of the development of Artificial Intelligence (AI) Ethics over the last 20 years using bibliometric methods. The study identifies three distinct phases: an incubation phase, a phase where AI is made human-like, and a phase where AI is made human-centric. The authors suggest that the next phase of AI Ethics will focus on making AI more machine-like as AI surpasses human capabilities. The findings highlight the rapid growth of AI Ethics, the shift in focus from making AI human-like to making it human-centric, and the growing concern over the ethical implications of AI, including bias, transparency, and accountability. The paper underscores the importance of AI Ethics in ensuring that AI technology is developed and deployed responsibly and that the general public's trust in AI is maintained. However, it also notes the limitations of the bibliometric approach and the potential for missing non-English literature.}
