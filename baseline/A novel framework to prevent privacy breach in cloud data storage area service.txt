The paper introduces a layered framework for cloud user privacy, combining onion and garlic privacy layers (OGPPA) to prevent data loss and ensure confidentiality. It emphasizes the importance of privacy in cloud computing, addressing the risks and challenges associated with data storage and access. The framework employs multiple encryption techniques to protect user data, making it difficult for attackers to intercept or misuse information. The paper highlights the need for robust privacy mechanisms in cloud environments and proposes OGPPA as a viable solution to safeguard user privacy in the context of cloud data storage. Key features include onion routing, garlic privacy, and the use of public-key cryptography for secure communication. The study acknowledges the limitations of existing approaches and aims to provide a comprehensive framework that addresses the complexities of cloud user privacy. The paper concludes by emphasizing the importance of ongoing research and development in enhancing privacy protection in cloud computing environments.}
