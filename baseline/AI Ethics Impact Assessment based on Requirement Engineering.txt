The paper introduces a methodology for evaluating the ethical impact of AI systems based on AI ethics guidelines. The authors analyze past ethical issues and assume that ethical risks can be linked to interactions between AI components and stakeholders. They propose an AI Ethics Impact Assessment, which involves constructing an AI ethics model to embody ethical guidelines and associating it with interactions using requirement engineering, and an impact assessment process. The methodology allows AI developers, providers, and users without AI ethics expertise to assess ethical risks at various stages of the AI lifecycle. The authors conducted an initial validation using a questionnaire survey, confirming the validity of their methodology and the need to improve verification methods in the future.
