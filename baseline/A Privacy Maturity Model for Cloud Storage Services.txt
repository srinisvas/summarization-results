The paper discusses the development of a Privacy Maturity Model (PMMCS) for cloud storage services to assess the maturity level of privacy provided by cloud storage providers. The proposed model is based on the CMMI-SVC framework and incorporates concepts from the BSIMM-CP model for evaluating compliance policies and governance. The paper addresses key issues such as identifying sensitive data, protecting access to sensitive data, and establishing a privacy level agreement between service providers and clients. It also considers compliance challenges, such as regional standards and high costs, and proposes a four-phase methodology for developing and evaluating the proposed model. The authors outline the importance of privacy in cloud computing and the need for a structured approach to ensure data privacy and compliance.}
