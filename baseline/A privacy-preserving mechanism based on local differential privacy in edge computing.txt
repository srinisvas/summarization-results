The paper proposes a privacy-preserving location data collection method for edge computing based on local differential privacy (LDP) and Voronoi diagrams. The method ensures that the privacy of users' location data is protected by perturbing the data within the Voronoi grids. The effectiveness of the proposed method is verified through comparison experiments with classical privacy-preserving mechanisms. The method has better privacy protection and higher data availability compared to existing mechanisms. However, it may consume more resources due to the need for generating multiple fake locations. Future work will focus on further improving the privacy-preserving model and its alignment with the self-characteristics of edge computing. The paper also explores the intersection of edge computing and blockchain, aiming to develop new and more effective privacy-preserving mechanisms.}
